Here’s a clear breakdown of the **Bernoulli water tank draining problem** explained in your transcript:

---

## 1. Problem Setup

We have a tank filled with water that drains through a hole at the bottom. The goal is to **calculate the time it takes to completely drain the tank**.

Key points:

* As the tank drains, the **height of the water decreases**.
* According to Bernoulli's principle, the **velocity of the fluid at the hole depends on the water height**.
* As the height decreases, the **drainage rate slows down**.

---

## 2. Small Volume Approach

The velocity (v) of water exiting the hole is initially given, but it changes with height. To handle this:

1. Consider an **infinitesimally small volume of water** (dV) exiting the hole in an infinitesimally small time (dt):

[
dV = A_{\text{hole}} \cdot v , dt
]

Where:

* (A_{\text{hole}}) = cross-sectional area of the hole
* (v) = discharge velocity

2. The volume change also corresponds to the **change in water height** in the tank:

[
dV = A_{\text{tank}} , dh
]

Where:

* (A_{\text{tank}}) = cross-sectional area of the tank
* (dh) = infinitesimal decrease in water height (negative)

---

## 3. Relating Height and Velocity

From **Torricelli’s law** (from Bernoulli’s principle):

[
v = \sqrt{2 g h}
]

Where:

* (g) = acceleration due to gravity
* (h) = instantaneous height of water above the hole

Substitute (v) into the infinitesimal volume equation:

[
A_{\text{hole}} \sqrt{2 g h} , dt = - A_{\text{tank}} , dh
]

The negative appears because (dh < 0) as water level decreases.

---

## 4. Setting Up the Differential Equation

[
dt = - \frac{A_{\text{tank}}}{A_{\text{hole}}} \frac{dh}{\sqrt{2 g h}}
]

To find total drain time (T), integrate from **initial height (H)** to **final height 0**:

[
T = \frac{A_{\text{tank}}}{A_{\text{hole}}} \int_0^H \frac{dh}{\sqrt{2 g h}}
]

---

## 5. Solve the Integral

[
\int_0^H \frac{dh}{\sqrt{h}} = 2 \sqrt{H}
]

So:

[
T = \frac{A_{\text{tank}}}{A_{\text{hole}}} \cdot \frac{2 \sqrt{H}}{\sqrt{2 g}}
]

[
\boxed{T = \frac{2 A_{\text{tank}} \sqrt{H}}{A_{\text{hole}} \sqrt{2 g}}}
]

---

## 6. Insights from the Result

* **Larger tank area** → longer drain time
* **Smaller hole area** → longer drain time
* **Doubling water height** does **not** double drain time because (T \propto \sqrt{H})
* The deeper the fluid, the faster the initial flow, which slightly offsets the extra volume.

---

This is the classic **Torricelli draining problem** solved using **calculus and Bernoulli’s principle**.
